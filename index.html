AI Aided GPS Indoor Positioning

Background
Voysys develops world-leading technology for teleoperation/remote operation. Customers in the logistics sector use the system to remotely operate forklift trucks in large warehouses. When a driver is assigned to a task, it may sometimes be with a new forklift in a new warehouse. To help the remote driver quickly understand his/her new task, a map of the warehouse with the forklifts current position is shown on the screen in front of the remote driver.
The large warehouses are very dynamic, and the owners are reluctant to complex positioning systems that require the installation of large amounts of nodes.

Goal
The primary goal of the thesis is to explore if a cost-efficient indoor positioning system can be developed based on GPS technology and if artificial intelligence can be used to make the installation of the system in a warehouse efficient and quick. A map of a warehouse visualizing the position of a forklift truck (or other moving objects) is expected as a demo/result.

Technology hypothesis
The hypothesis is that signals from several GPS nodes installed inside a warehouse could be processed through a neural network to make a good-enough positioning of the receiving device. By using a second positioning system during training, the neural network may be fed with data, and thus gradually become good enough at positioning within the warehouse.

Tests in real warehouses - cooperation with Tech Team Norrköping
The work will be carried out in cooperation with several companies in Norrköping within the concept “Tech Team Norrköping”. Holmen has promised to participate in a warehouse for tests, and we hope to get access to one or two additional warehouses. HIQ will participate with their experiences from similar projects in the past.
